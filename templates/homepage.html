The Darsanians' Houses

Darsanians Houses, inspired by animals mentioned in the Qur’an.

Al-Ghuraab

Inspired by the crow sent by Allah to teach Qabil how to bury his brother. Symbolizes awareness and learning from signs. (Qur’an 5:31)

An-Nahl

Inspired by the bee, known for discipline, cooperation, and producing goodness. Represents teamwork and productivity. (Qur’an 16:68–69)

An-Nun

Inspired by the great fish associated with Prophet Yunus (AS). Represents patience, repentance, and trust in Allah. (Qur’an 21:87)

Al-Adiyat

Inspired by the charging horses mentioned in Surah Al-Adiyat. Symbolizes strength, loyalty, and relentless effort. (Qur’an 100:1–5)

Al-Hudhud

Inspired by the hoopoe bird in the story of Prophet Sulaiman (AS). Represents intelligence, communication, and seeking truth. (Qur’an 27:20–22)

An-Naml

Inspired by the ants who warned one another of danger. Represents leadership, foresight, and community care. (Qur’an 27:18)
